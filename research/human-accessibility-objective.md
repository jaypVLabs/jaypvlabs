# Human Accessibility Objective

**Status:** Research ethics and direction record

## Core Statement

Everyone should have the opportunity to gain knowledge, be creative, and enjoy life while we have it.

## Research Meaning

The Recursive Accessibility and Balance framework should be tested as a way to understand how systems expand or restrict access to valid futures.

The purpose is not to claim that access solves every human problem.

The purpose is to test whether improving valid accessibility changes the probability of better outcomes across domains.

## Key Distinctions

### Observation vs. Explanation

An observable state is not the full explanation.

### Symptom vs. Mechanism

A symptom is evidence.

A mechanism produces the evidence.

### Person vs. Circumstance

A person's current behavior should be evaluated with attention to state, environment, constraints, memory, opportunity, and available transitions.

### Accessibility vs. Outcome

Accessibility increases reachability.

It does not guarantee a fixed result.

## Research Questions

1. How does increased access to knowledge change future outcome probability?
2. How does creative access affect individual and community resilience?
3. How does access to care affect recovery probability?
4. How does environmental instability restrict valid futures?
5. How can systems expand accessibility without creating imbalance?
6. How can evidence distinguish intent, behavior, circumstance, and constraint?

## Final Rule

The framework should be evaluated by whether it helps identify valid ways to expand human accessibility while preserving balance.
